✅ Messaging Do's & Don'ts

Do's

  • Show deep empathy for the exhaustion and helplessness of midnight caregivers.
  • Emphasize "no brewing needed" and "juice-flavor taste" — this solves the #1 problem of buying medicine that nobody will take.
  • Paint vivid, gut-wrenching scenes (e.g., the humiliation of coughing in a meeting, being jolted awake by midnight coughing).
  • Position the product as a "premium immune shield" that protects the whole family.
  • Do the math for them — show them TMK saves money vs. hospital visits, missed work, and wasted supplements.
  • Use strong comparisons (TMK's delicious, effective liquid vs. bitter TCM / useless Western syrup).

Don'ts

  • Never use "miracle cure," "cures everything," or "completely eliminates asthma in X days" — these are fraudulent claims.
  • Never make the brand look like a cheap supermarket throat lozenge or sugar water.
  • For smokers — never lecture, judge, or demand they quit immediately. That makes them reject everything.
  • Don't use obscure medical jargon that ordinary people can't understand. Keep it simple.
  • Don't just say "stops coughing" — explain HOW it cleans the lungs and eliminates the root inflammation.
  • Never use disgusting, gory images of diseased lungs — it repulses audiences and they'll scroll away.

⚖️ Claims & Compliance — Read Before Running Ads

Wellous is very strict about compliance. Because Wellous sells dietary supplements, there are strict rules about what you can and cannot say.

You CAN Say

  • Helps soothe the throat and reduce coughing.
  • Traditionally used for lung and respiratory wellness.
  • The liquid is easy to drink and children love the taste.
  • Contains natural plant-based ingredients: Tiger Milk Mushroom, Licorice, and Mandarin Orange.
  • A great daily wellness companion for smokers and people with chronic cough.

You CANNOT Say

  • "100% cures asthma, pneumonia, or any serious/terminal illness" — say "long-term use can effectively manage asthma and help prevent pneumonia" instead.
  • "Miracle drug," "medical miracle," or any exaggerated, too-good-to-be-true language.
  • "Drink this and you can stop taking your doctor's medication" — position it as complementary: use medication for acute control, TMK for long-term lung nourishment.
  • "Drink this and you can smoke as much as you want without getting sick."
⚠️ Strict Warning
Never use disgusting, gory images. Never call it a "miracle cure for everything." Protect the brand's premium image at all times.
🚫 Banned Words & Phrases
Miracle drug, cures everything, lung cancer killer, 100% eradication, instant results, medical miracle, cheap, budget alternative.
⚠️ Required Disclaimer on All Ads
"This product is a dietary supplement and is not intended to replace medical treatment. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Consult a healthcare professional if you have a medical condition."
